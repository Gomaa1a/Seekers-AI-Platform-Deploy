## pages_show_list
**Description:**
After a business user connects their Facebook account via Facebook Login, Seekers
AI displays the list of
Facebook Pages they manage so they can choose which Page to connect to our app.
This permission is required to render that Page-selection step during onboarding;
without it the user cannot pick which Page our support automation should manage.

**Test call:** `GET /me/accounts`

---

## pages_read_engagement
**Description:**
Seekers AI reads the connected Page's posts and the comments on them so our AI
support agent can detect new customer comments and generate accurate, in-context
replies. We also read aggregate Page engagement to display recent activity in the
business's analytics dashboard. Data is used only to operate the support and
analytics features for that business.

**Test call:** `GET /{page-id}?fields=fan_count,posts{message,comments}`

---

## pages_read_user_content
**Description:**
Seekers AI reads user-generated content on the connected Page — visitor comments
and posts — so the AI agent can read each customer comment and respond, and so the
business admin can moderate that content from our dashboard. We only access content
on Pages the user has explicitly connected.

**Test call:** `GET /{page-id}/feed` or `GET /{post-id}/comments`

---

## pages_manage_metadata
**Description:**
Seekers AI uses this permission to subscribe the connected Page to our webhook so
we receive real-time message and comment events. We call the Page's
`subscribed_apps` endpoint to register (and unregister on disconnect) the webhook
subscription. This is required for the app to receive any incoming messages.

**Test call:** `POST /{page-id}/subscribed_apps?subscribed_fields=messages,feed`

---

## pages_manage_engagement
**Description:**
Seekers AI replies to and moderates comments on the connected Page's posts on the
admin's behalf — posting public replies generated by the AI agent and hiding or
removing spam — as part of our comment auto-reply and moderation feature.

**Test call:** `POST /{comment-id}/comments` with `message=Thanks for reaching out!`

---

## pages_messaging
**Description:**
This is the core feature of Seekers AI. We receive customer messages sent to the
connected Page's Messenger inbox via webhook, and send AI-generated replies through
the Send API on behalf of the Page, respecting Messenger Platform policy (e.g. the
24-hour standard messaging window). This lets businesses automate first-line
customer support on Messenger.

**Test call:** `POST /{page-id}/messages` with a `recipient` who has messaged the
Page (send a message to the Page first from a test user, then reply via the API).

**Reproduction instructions (this card asks for them explicitly):**
> 1. Log in to Seekers AI at `https://www.seekersai.org` with the test account
>    (`[TEST_EMAIL]` / `[TEST_PASSWORD]`); a Facebook Page is already connected.
> 2. From a different Facebook account, open the connected Page and send it a

> 3. Within a few seconds Seekers AI receives the message via webhook and the AI
>    agent sends an automated reply back through the Send API.

# TOP-LEVEL SECTIONS

These are the three cards shown on the main submission page
(Allowed usage / Data handling / Reviewer instructions).

## 1. Allowed usage
> Seekers AI is an omnichannel customer-support platform for businesses. A business
> creates an account, connects a Facebook Page and its linked Instagram
> Professional account via Facebook Login, and our app then receives customer messages and comments
> through Meta webhooks and replies on the business's behalf using an AI agent, while
> showing engagement analytics in a dashboard.
>
> We use the requested Page and Instagram permissions solely to operate this support
> automation for the businesses that explicitly connect their own assets: reading
> incoming messages/comments, sending replies, subscribing the Page to our webhook,
> and reading engagement to populate the dashboard. We do not sell data, do not use
> message content for advertising, and access data only for connected accounts.

## 2. Data handling
Use these answers for the data-handling questions (adjust to the exact wording Meta shows):

- **What data do you collect?** Business account profile (name and email from our own
  registration form; public profile from Facebook Login when they connect Meta assets —
  we do not request the `email` scope); Meta connection data (Page IDs, Instagram account IDs, Page
  access tokens stored encrypted); message and comment content plus sender PSIDs for
  connected accounts; usage/engagement metrics and technical logs.
- **How is it used?** Only to receive and reply to messages/comments on behalf of the
  connecting business and to show that business its own analytics. Replies are generated
  by our AI processor (Anthropic / Claude).
- **Is it stored?** Yes — in our PostgreSQL database hosted on Railway. Access tokens are
  encrypted with AES-256-GCM. Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS).
- **Shared with third parties?** Only sub-processors required to run the service: cloud
  hosting (Railway) and our AI model provider (Anthropic). No sale of data.
- **Retention & deletion?** Retained while the account is active; deleted on disconnect or
  account deletion. Automated via our Meta deletion callback, or by request.
- **Privacy Policy URL:** `https://YOUR_FRONTEND_DOMAIN/privacy-policy.html`
- **Data Deletion Request URL (callback):** `https://PUBLIC_BACKEND_DOMAIN/api/meta/deletion`
- **Deauthorize callback URL:** `https://PUBLIC_BACKEND_DOMAIN/api/meta/deauthorize`
- **User-facing deletion instructions:** `https://YOUR_FRONTEND_DOMAIN/data-deletion.html`
